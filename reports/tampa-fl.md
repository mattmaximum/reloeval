# Tampa, FL — Relocation Report

*Hillsborough County · Generated 2026-07-24*

Tampa, FL presents a mixed relocation profile with compelling economic and quality-of-life benefits tempered by significant environmental and safety concerns. The city's primary draws include no state income tax, excellent public education (90.9 district rating), world-class healthcare facilities including Tampa General Hospital, and robust food infrastructure with abundant grocery and farmers market options serving over 2 million residents. Geographic accessibility is strong via Tampa International Airport and proximity to Orlando within 6 hours. However, potential residents must carefully weigh substantial risks: the area's extreme vulnerability to hurricanes and flooding due to its sea-level coastal location (2.5 miles from ocean), current Stage 3 high drought conditions with the main reservoir at critically low 14.7% capacity and strict water restrictions already in place, and elevated violent crime rates at 445 per 100K residents. The tropical climate offers year-round growing potential but limited annual sunshine (250 days), while electricity costs are reasonable at 13.5 cents/kWh though solar installation is constrained by weak solar score (18.0). The community is politically conservative, car-dependent with moderate public transit, and while housing is relatively affordable compared to other Florida markets (median $445K), the high prevalence of HOA-governed communities (1,800+) and permissive gun laws reflect a libertarian political orientation. Tampa is best suited for retirees or professionals prioritizing no state income tax and healthcare access who are willing to accept hurricane risk, current water scarcity, and a car-dependent lifestyle in exchange for reasonable affordability and strong amenities.

## Water Supply & Security

Bill Young) at critically low 14.7% capacity, though water supply is currently bolstered by multiple backup sources including desalination and aquifer storage—but strict watering restrictions (one day/week) are already in effect under an Extreme Water Shortage Order.*

**Pros:** Multiple backup water sources (desalination, aquifer storage, canal), City has legal permits for authorized withdrawals up to 120 MGD peak
**Cons:** Severe drought with reservoir at 14.7% capacity, Extreme water shortage restrictions already active, 14.46-inch rainfall deficit
