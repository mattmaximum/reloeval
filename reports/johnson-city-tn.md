# Johnson City, TN — Relocation Report

*Washington · Generated 2026-07-24*

Johnson City, Tennessee presents a compelling relocation destination for those prioritizing affordability, safety, and quality of life in a mid-sized community setting. With a median home price of $281,433, zero state income tax, and remarkably zero reported violent and property crimes, the city offers exceptional financial and personal security advantages, complemented by solid education through 12 public schools rated 4.0 and outstanding healthcare access via a Level 1 Trauma Center within Ballad Health's extensive 21-hospital network. The temperate zone 7b climate delivers a reasonable 190-day growing season with mild winters and warm summers, though limited sunshine (201 days annually) and moderate-to-high drought risk from surface water reliance warrant consideration. While the city benefits from low natural disaster risk (hurricanes, earthquakes) and reasonable air quality, prospective residents should note that daily living requires a car due to limited transit, and the moderate food infrastructure lacks major chains like Whole Foods or Costco. Infrastructure strengths include reliable electricity costs at 11.82¢/kWh and strong gun rights protections, while regional connectivity is solid via Tri-Cities Regional Airport and Interstate 26, though Atlanta—the nearest major metropolitan hub—requires a 2.5-hour drive. Overall, Johnson City appeals most to cost-conscious professionals and families seeking a safe, affordable community with excellent healthcare and education but who can adapt to car-dependent living and limited urban amenities.
