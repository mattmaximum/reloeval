# Flagstaff, AZ — Relocation Report

*Coconino · Generated 2026-07-24*

Flagstaff, Arizona presents a compelling relocation destination for those seeking a vibrant college town with a temperate mountain climate, excellent civic services, and strong infrastructure. Located at 7,000 feet elevation near Phoenix, this community of 77,260 combines a diverse, educated population with notably low crime rates and strong public schools, supported by a robust healthcare system anchored by a trauma center. The area benefits from a certified 100-year water supply from multiple sources, moderate electricity costs (12.5¢/kWh) with growing grid reliability, and solid broadband connectivity with good walkability (72), while proximity to major airports enables convenient regional travel. However, potential relocators should carefully weigh significant environmental and climate trade-offs: the region faces high wildfire risk (experiencing 22 smoke days annually) with moderate-to-high flood exposure, endures a harsh winter climate (USDA Zone 5b) with a short 113-day growing season and extended frost seasons, and offers a considerably higher median home price ($450k) coupled with moderate-to-strict development permitting. While the economic fundamentals and quality of life amenities are solid, Flagstaff's appeal ultimately depends on whether the outdoor recreation and cultural benefits of a mountain college town outweigh the constraints of its cold climate, natural hazards, housing costs, and limited specialty retail access.
