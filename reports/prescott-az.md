# Prescott, AZ — Relocation Report

*Yavapai County · Generated 2026-07-24*

Prescott, Arizona presents a compelling but trade-off-heavy relocation destination for those seeking a small-town lifestyle at elevation near Phoenix. This charming community of 49,000 residents offers significant advantages: a safe, retiree-friendly environment (median age 59.7, violent crime 2.8 per 100K), reliable water security backed by a 100-year Assured Water Supply designation and substantial groundwater reserves, excellent solar potential with favorable net metering policies, solid healthcare infrastructure including a Level 4 Trauma Center, and reasonable accessibility with a $550K median home price and good broadband connectivity. The mild winters and 324 sunny days annually appeal to those fleeing harsh climates, while the moderate growing season and no-test homeschooling flexibility attract families with agricultural or educational flexibility. However, prospective relocators must carefully weigh the region's significant natural hazard exposure—particularly the very high wildfire risk (25 smoke days yearly) and moderate-to-high flood risk—against quality-of-life benefits. Additional considerations include above-average electricity costs (16.4¢/kWh), limited public transit and moderate walkability, the older demographic profile, and reliance on a 2-hour drive to Phoenix Sky Harbor for major regional travel. Prescott suits retirees, remote workers, and those prioritizing safety and environmental resilience over urban amenities, but demands careful evaluation of personal risk tolerance regarding natural hazards.

## Water Supply & Security

*Water supply relies on deep groundwater (250 ft depth) supplemented by surface reservoirs and reclaimed water, with Assured Water Supply designation guaranteeing 100-year availability. The city operates within Arizona's Prescott Active Management Area requiring conservation and renewable source replacement, but holds substantial backup capacity of 16,507 acre-feet annually plus legal rights to import 8,068 acre-feet from Big Chino Sub-basin.*

**Pros:** 100-year water supply guarantee (DAWS), Substantial backup capacity: 16.5k acre-feet annually, Diversified sources: groundwater, reservoirs, reclaimed water, import rights
**Cons:** Deep wells required: 250 ft to water table, Mandatory conservation & renewable replacement rules
