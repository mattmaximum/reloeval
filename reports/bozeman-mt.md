# Bozeman, MT — Relocation Report

*Gallatin · Generated 2026-07-24*

Bozeman, Montana presents a compelling relocation destination for those prioritizing natural amenities, safety, and community character, though it demands careful consideration of environmental and financial constraints. The mid-sized mountain city (~59,000 residents) excels in public safety (exceptionally low violent crime at 2.3 per 100k), educational quality (strong schools and Montana State University), and healthcare infrastructure (Level III Trauma Center with 270+ providers), while offering distinctive advantages like constitutional carry rights, legal raw dairy access, year-round farmers markets, and excellent regional air connectivity. However, prospective residents must weigh significant drawbacks: acute water vulnerability with the region in its sixth consecutive drought year and already consuming 60% of reliable supply, substantial earthquake risk (94.1/100 FEMA score), high wildfire exposure (~30 smoke days annually), and considerable financial barriers including a median home price of $592,600 combined with a 13.75% tax burden and limited access to major retail chains. The high elevation (4,817 ft) location near Yellowstone Caldera offers stunning natural beauty and low hurricane risk but delivers limited sunshine (121 days annually), minimal solar potential (3.7 score), and despite cold winters, surprisingly little snowfall. Best suited for those prioritizing outdoor lifestyle, small-town community, and safety over urban convenience and financial affordability, Bozeman requires adaptability to water conservation constraints and willingness to engage with local food systems and supply chains.

## Water Supply & Security

*Bozeman relies almost entirely on snowpack-fed surface water (80% from mountain creeks), making it highly vulnerable to drought—the city consumed 60% of its reliable supply in 2024 during drought conditions while the region faces its sixth consecutive drought year with only 16 inches annual precipitation. However, the city has proactive management tools, a drought plan with tiered restrictions, and backup storage capacity in Hyalite Reservoir.*

**Pros:** Hyalite Reservoir backup storage at full capacity, Proactive forecasting with Supply Optimization Tool, Drought plan with tiered conservation measures, 5-year expansion projects adding 1,575 acre-feet
**Cons:** High drought risk, 6th consecutive drought year, Semi-arid climate, only 16 inches precipitation annually, Already used 60% of reliable supply in 2024 drought, 80% dependent on snowpack, vulnerable to earlier snowmelt
