# Penrose, CO — Relocation Report

*Fremont County · Generated 2026-07-24*

Penrose, Colorado presents a compelling relocation destination for those prioritizing natural beauty, favorable climate, and community safety over urban amenities and convenience. The 4,087-person town offers an exceptionally safe environment with zero violent and property crime rates, a mild climate with 290 sunshine days and a 176-day growing season ideal for year-round outdoor enthusiasts and gardeners, plus reasonable housing costs ($428,600 median) and strong solar potential with favorable net metering policies. However, prospective residents must carefully weigh significant trade-offs: critical water supply challenges with Phase 1 voluntary curtailment already in effect and ongoing litigation over water rights; minimal educational infrastructure with only one below-average school necessitating homeschooling or long commutes; substantial healthcare access limitations with the nearest trauma center 45 miles away; and a car-dependent lifestyle with no major retail chains locally, requiring regular trips to Colorado Springs or Pueblo for grocery shopping. The area's high elevation (5,430 ft), combined with 28 annual wildfire smoke days and high flood risk, demands consideration of natural hazard resilience. Penrose suits remote workers, retirees, or families seeking a quiet, safe mountain community with excellent gardening conditions and democratic-leaning politics, but is poorly suited for those requiring proximity to specialized healthcare, diverse educational options, convenient shopping, or climate resilience against water scarcity.
